Briards

a heart wrapped in fur

The briard, or berger de brie, is a large breed of dog traditionally used as a herder and guardian of sheep.

Breed History

The briad, which is believed to have originationed in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and protect sheet. The breed was used by the French Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers becauase of its fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the First World War almost to the point of extinction. Currently the population of briads is slowly recovering. Charlemagne, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson, and Lafayette all owned briards.
